Monday marked the official start of the high school fall sports season, meaning no more captain's practices, no more club practices and, most likely, no more lazy afternoons binge-watching Netflix programming.
Many teams started practicing at 8 a.m. sharp.
"No walking," shouted first-year soccer coach Rob St. Clair. "There's no walking in soccer."
The Eden Prairie High School girls tennis team, under the leadership of first-year head coach Alexa Horoshak, opens Eden Prairie's competitive season on Monday with a 9 a.m. away match at Lakeville North.
The defending state champion Eden Prairie football team plays its season opener on Thursday, Aug. 30, at Eastview. It plays its home opener on Friday, Sept. 14 (Prior Lake)
