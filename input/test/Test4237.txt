How Atal Bihari Vajpayee shaped India's future 3102 views Aug 17, 2018, 10:14 IST How Atal Bihari Vajpayee shaped India's future Aug 17, 2018, 10:14 IST Source: GIST Fiscal prudence: The Fiscal Responsibility Act was introduced to bring down fiscal deficit. Vajpayee also boosted public sector savings and was instrumental for India's GDP touching the eight per cent mark in his tenure. Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan: It was launched in 2000 to provide free education to children between aged between 6 and 14 years. This was instrumental in leading to a major decline in school dropout rates. Foreign diplomacy: Vajpayee inaugurated the Delhi-Lahore bus service in 1999 and travelled in it to meet Pak PM Nawaz Sharif. In 2000, Bill Clinton became the first US President to visit India after 1978. India improved trade and reduced territorial disputes with China. Infrastructure: He launched the Golden Quadrilateral project which connected major cities through a network of national highways. The Pradhanmantri Gramin Sadak Yojna was aimed at connecting distant villages to major towns. Pokhran: India undertook five underground nuclear bomb explosions in 1998. This led to US and European Union sanctions but failed to have any major negative effect on the Indian economy. Kargil war: Indian Armed Forces won a decisive victory by evicting Pakistani soldiers and Kashmiri militants from India across the LoC between May and July, 1999. Scientific temper: Vajpayee increased government spending on scientific research. This paved the way for clearance to the Chandrayaan-I project. India eventually reached the moon in 2008
